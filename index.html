01 Mathematical Surrogate

In silicon pixel detectors like MALTA2, small pixels are grouped into cluster units of size \(N_x \times N_y\). This introduces a crucial optimization trade-off between digital merging coincidence noise and timing cuts:

\[\Phi(N_x, N_y) = \frac{L_x \, p_x}{N_x} + \frac{L_y \, p_y}{N_y}\]

Here, \(p_x, p_y\) represent the coincidence probabilities of digital boundaries, while \(L_x, L_y\) capture the timing or signal survival rates of horizontal and vertical propagation.

03 Physics Regimes Analyzed
● Isotropic Regime

Under symmetric coincidence probabilities and uniform survival rates (\(\chi = 1.0\)), the absolute optimal cluster aspect ratio is a square layout (\(Y = 0 \implies 8 \times 8\)), minimizing total boundary merges uniformly.

● Survivable-X Regime

When horizontal signals face timing degradation or encoding cuts (\(\chi \le 0.25\)), the continuous optimal layout shifts dynamically towards tall, high-aspect structures (like \(2 \times 32\) or \(4 \times 16\)), saving up to 30% in noise metrics.
